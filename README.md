3. Clinical Presentation Findings

Common Compulsions (Behaviors)

The five most common types of compulsions are widely distributed, with some behaviors slightly more prevalent than others:

Most Common: Counting and Washing are the most frequently reported compulsions, both nearing the maximum count shown on the chart.

Highly Prevalent: Checking, Praying, and Ordering follow closely, all registering high counts. This suggests that treatment programs should broadly address the full range of common ritualistic behaviors.

Obsession Counts (Thoughts)

Obsessive themes are also highly common, with two types clearly leading the presentation:

Leading Obsessions: Harm-related and Contamination obsessions are the most common intrusive thought patterns reported by patients.

Other Obsessions: Religious, Symmetry, and Hoarding are also highly prevalent, clustering slightly lower than the top two but still affecting a large number of patients.

Conclusion

The analytics suggest a slight female majority in the OCD population, with Caucasian and Hispanic patients forming the largest ethnic groups. Clinically, the data highlights a high co-occurrence and prevalence across almost all major compulsion and obsession types, though Counting/Washing (compulsions) and Harm-related/Contamination (obsessions) stand out as primary focus areas. The patient load peaked in 2018 and has been decreasing since, which warrants further investigation into operational changes or external factors influencing patient intake.
